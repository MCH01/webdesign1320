Aftermath of the Battle

While many people we're wounded during the course of the battle, no one was killed. Rebellions in nearby Meigs and Monroe county flared up, sadly with the death of one Sheriff happening.
The GI Non-Partisan League won a landslide victory. 2,175 votes for Knox Henry, 1,270 for Cantrell. The prior adminstration and deputies we're replaced. The Mayor resigned as well and the county political machine had fallen.
Bill White was made a Sheriff's Deputy under the GI canidate for Sheriff, Knox Henry. Deputy Wise who shot Tom Gillespie became the only person who took part in the battle to be charged. He was sentenced to three years but only served one.
Veterans in other Tennessee counties facing the same issue attempted to form a statewide coalition against the Crump political machine. Eventually the GI governement in Athens collapsed. Statewide, the GI movement faded and politics returned to normal.
