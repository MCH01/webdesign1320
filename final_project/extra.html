Extra Bit's Of Info

The Antonine Plague ranks as the 8th deadliest epidemic in human history. In the Roman world there would be a much worse plague in the Justinian Plague, which would wipe out 7-56% of the world population, making it the 3rd deadliest epidemic. As stated before you have to be careful when reading on these Emperor's, many of the historians to write about them that we think of ancient historians (Cassius Dio for example) we're not around for their reigns, some writing about it centuries later. While these five men are named as the Five Good Emperors of Rome, they we're not the only ones considered good. Cases can be made for Emperor's such as Constantine, Aurelian, or Augustus could all be considered greater than even these Emperor's.
The maps you see below you are from the beginning (Nerva's Reign), middle (Hadrian's Reign), and end (Marcus Aurelius's Reign) of the Five Good Emperors.
