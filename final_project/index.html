Who We're They?

The five good Emperors of Rome we're an almost mythical lineup of what many historians would consider the best Roman Emperors. Their combined rule lasted from the years 96, to 180. These five Emperors names were Nerva, Trajan, Hadrian, Antoninus Pius, and Marcus Aurelius. The most recognizable name will likely be Marcus Aurelius thanks to the movie gladiator, and his literature works which can still be read today. During their rule, Rome was arguably at it's most stable, and without a doubt at it's largest extent with Trajan's conquests in 117. The main reason they are considered the five good emperors, however, is the prosperity and peace Rome had during their rule, something that both before and after their time was uncommon to have for extended periods of time. This website should give you a basic insight into these Emperors, but the history surrounding them is extremely interesting and there are plenty of resources to teach you more about this fascinating period of history.

A Note

When it comes to ancient history, and that of Roman history, one has to keep in mind that the historians from these times are not like the historians of today. Many accounts that we have from this time period are filled with lies or deceit, and you should take any info you read about with a massive grain of salt.
